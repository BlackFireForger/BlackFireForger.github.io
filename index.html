INTRO

Catalytic convertors based on the use of platinum, palladium and rhodium play a major role in the cleaning of automobile emissions. Gold, when dispersed as nano-sized particles, has demonstrated significant activity in the conversion of toxic components, including carbon monoxide, unburnt hydrocarbons and nitrogen oxides, in engine emissions and some advantages over the platinum group metals.

PRIOR DISADVANTAGES

GNP-based catalysts have a few needs for automobile emission control. On one hand, the cold-start temperature of an engine is insufficient to achieve a high catalytic efficiency for the conversion of pollutants. On the other hand, the normal temperature of the exhaust after the start-up period is too high for GNPs stability

THE CHALLENGES

We understand the challenges related to cold-start temperatures and the stability of GNPs at high exhaust temperatures. To address these concerns, our research and development team has successfully devised an innovative workaround to optimize catalytic efficiency and enhance GNP stability throughout the entire operational range of an automobile. Hence we have come with set a set of tasks to address each problem.

TASK

During the cold-start phase, the thermal management system actively promotes a rapid temperature increase within the catalytic converter, ensuring that the GNPs reach their optimal operating temperature quickly. This accelerates the activation of the catalytic process, effectively reducing emissions even during the initial stages of engine operation.
